El [taoísmo o daoísmo](https://es.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tao%C3%ADsmo) (en chino, 道教; pinyin, dàojiào; literalmente, ‘enseñanza del camino’) es una religión no teísta tradición filosófica y espiritual de origen chino la cual enfatiza vivir en armonía con el tao (la ‘vía’ o el ‘camino’).

# Cosas con las que resueno

- Throughout the history of Taoism, mountains have occupied a special place for Taoist practice. They are seen as sacred spaces and as the ideal places for Taoist cultivation and Taoist monastic or eremitic life, which may include "cloud wandering" (yunyou) in the mountains and dwelling in mountain hermitages (an) or grottoes (dong)
- [Búsqueda de la longevidad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taoism#:~:text=Taoist%20longevity%20methods%20are%20closely%20related%20to%20ancient%20Chinese%20medicine) a través de procesos naturales:
  - [Key elements of these "nourishing life" (yangsheng) methods include: moderation in all things (drink, food, etc.), adapting to the cycles of the seasons by following injunctions regarding healing exercises (daoyin), and breathwork](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taoism#:~:text=The%20goal%20of,.%5B274)

# Diferencia entre taoismo filosófico y taoismo religioso

[Taoist philosophy](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taoist_philosophy#:~:text=Taoist%20philosophy%20(Chinese%3A%20%E9%81%93%E5%AE%B6%3B%20pinyin%3A%20D%C3%A0oji%C4%81%3B%20lit.%20%27Tao%20school%27)%2C%20also%20known%20as%20Taology%20or%20philosophical%20Taoism%20(to%20distinguish%20it%20from%20religious%20Taoism)%20is%20a%20set%20of%20various%20philosophical%20currents%20of%20Taoism%2C%20a%20tradition%20of%20Chinese%20origin%20that%20emphasizes%20living%20in%20harmony%20with%20the%20D%C3%A0o>) also known as Taology or philosophical Taoism (to distinguish it from religious Taoism) is a set of various philosophical currents of Taoism, a tradition of Chinese origin that emphasizes living in harmony with the Dào
